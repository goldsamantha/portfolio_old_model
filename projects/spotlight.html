Spotlight

Website and Mobile App

Spotlight is a social contest app that allows users to compete on a real-time leaderboard to score the most likes on posts and earn rewards.

The key differentiator of the app is the leaderboard page. It is crucial for this page to be immediately informative and engaging. Initially the app had a static leaderboard that showcased the top ten contestants, but didn't reveal much more information. As the app grew so did the demand for contests, and it was clear the app needed some re-thinking. I led a major redesign to refresh the app and improve functionality for showcasing multiple contests.

The new leaderboard needed to showcase and differentiate multiple contests and leaders in those challenges. After many iterations and tests with toggling pages to navigate through a list of contests, I decided to divide the leaderboard two distinct pages — one that showed a list of multiple contests and one that showcased details from a contest. This allows easy access for users who wanted to view a specific contest's page with a single tap, rather than toggling through a number of pages before finding the right one.

In the new detail view, we highlight more information about the contestants, primarily those in the lead. In previous versions of the app the leaderboard had no visual assets and was simply a list of leading contestants from 1 to 100. The new detailed contest view makes it easy to identify the winners while saving valuable screen real estate. The new view enables the user to follow winning contestants directly from the contest page. Since those players tended to be the most popular in the app, this removed the step of clicking into their profile page and then following.

Before the re-design there was no central hub that encorportated all data relevant to a contest. In other words the leaderboard showed winners but no other information. I added a section for a contest description and image as well as a stream so that users could actually see what posts were being entered into a contest.

Along with adding new pages and components for the leaderboard, I refreshed the UI for the Spotlight App from the MVP model to maturity. After completing wireframes and restructuring the sign-up flow to make the onboarding process easier, I adapted the visuals. I lightened the header color, updated the app's navigation icons, and improved the feed with a card-layout which differentiated each post from one another and showcased the most relevant detials of a post such as author, title, and time-posted. I also adapted the notifications page to provide more meaningful alerts. For instance, I added notifications to be sent to users when they, or someone they follow, placed in the top three. I modified the UI of the page as well from a single list that only had text to a layout which featured imagery to make notifications from friends and followers more rapidly recognizeable.
